Economic factor correlation with TX rebar demand
TX Building PermitsStrong correlation
r = +0.940–1 month lead
Strongest single predictor. Permit volumes translate almost directly into rebar orders — each 1,000-unit monthly increase historically drives ~8,500 tons of additional TX demand within 60 days. The 2021 surge accurately predicted the 2022 demand peak.
TX Construction GDPStrong correlation
r = +0.91Coincident
Best concurrent validator. TX construction GDP (FRED: TXCONSTNGSP) moves in near lockstep with rebar demand. Quarterly swings of ±5% correspond to ±4–6% rebar volume changes. Best used to confirm trends already signaled by permits.
WTI Crude Oil PriceStrong correlation
r = +0.763–6 month lead
Critical Texas-specific driver. Oil price drives energy-sector construction (pipelines, plants, LNG terminals) which is uniquely rebar-intensive. The 2015–16 crash drove a 10% demand drop; the 2021–22 rebound to $95 drove the 2022 demand peak.
TxDOT Contract AwardsStrong correlation
r = +0.726–12 month lead
Best long-range signal. TxDOT awards precede rebar consumption by 6–12 months as projects mobilize. With $39.9B committed for FY26–27, this points to elevated infrastructure demand through 2027–28 even if residential softens.
TX Population GrowthModerate correlation
r = +0.6812–18 month lag
Structural demand floor. Texas added ~500K residents in 2024. Population growth drives single-family starts with an 18-month lag, providing a durable slow-moving baseline for long-range demand forecasting.
10-Year Treasury YieldModerate correlation
r = -0.613–6 month lead
Negative correlation — rising rates dampen demand. The 2022–23 rate hike cycle caused the sharpest single-year residential permit decline in TX since 2009. Each 100bps rise historically reduces TX residential rebar demand by ~6–8%.
HMS Scrap Steel PriceWeak correlation
r = +0.48Coincident
Noisy supply-side indicator. High scrap prices can both reflect strong demand and suppress it by raising finished rebar prices. More useful as a mill margin indicator than a demand predictor.
ISM Manufacturing PMIWeak correlation
r = +0.441–2 month lead
Weak Texas-specific signal. TX demand is driven more by energy and infrastructure than broad manufacturing cycles. Most useful at extremes (below 45 or above 55); middle readings have low predictive value for TX specifically.

Demand index = composite proxy (TX construction GDP + permits + rebar price), normalized 2019=100. Correlation coefficients vs. TX construction GDP (FRED: TXCONSTNGSP) 2015–2024. Live data auto-refreshed every Monday 6 AM CT via GitHub Actions.
